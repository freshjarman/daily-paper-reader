Abstract. We study the correlations of the celebrated Sine Œ≤ point process. This point process arises as the bulk scaling limit of Œ≤-ensembles and has a geometric description through the Brownian carousel, as shown by Valk√≥ and Vir√°g (2009). We establish that the averaged k-point truncated correlation functions decay polyno-mially in the limit of large separation. We show that the decay exponent is of order 1/Œ≤ 

for large Œ≤. This is a step towards a conjecture by Forrester and Haldane regarding the exact asymptotics of the two-point correlation function, a problem recently addressed by Qu and Valk√≥ (2025). Our proofs, which rely on a careful analysis of the coupling of dif-fusions associated with the Brownian carousel, hold for all Œ≤ > 0 and k ‚â• 1, significantly extending previous results limited to specific values of Œ≤ or k.
